How I approach growth

Growth is a business problem, not a marketing problem.

Most companies already have enough channels, tools, and tactics. What they lack is alignment between product, positioning, acquisition, and revenue.

My work starts by identifying the bottleneck.
Then building systems that remove it.

The goal isn't more activity.
The goal is predictable growth.

Start with the bottleneck
More channels and more spend rarely fix the real problem. I look for where growth is actually stalling before recommending anything.
Build for repeatability
A campaign is a one-time event. A growth system is an asset. Everything I build is meant to keep working after I'm done.
Measure what moves revenue
Vanity metrics are easy to produce. I focus on CAC, LTV, conversion rates, and pipeline — numbers that connect to business outcomes.
Move fast, adjust often
Most decisions don't need a quarter of research. I run structured experiments, learn quickly, and scale what works.
